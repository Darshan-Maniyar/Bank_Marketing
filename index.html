Bank: Telemarketing for Deposit


A bank in Portugal was rolling out a new term deposits product for its existing customers. They wanted to understand which of their existing customers to call and target for this new product so that ROI would be high. In the past they had connected to their customer base through phone calls for various other products. Results for these previous campaigns had been made available for over 30,000 customers.

Data Summary:

  • y (subscribed for deposit): This is the dependent (target) variable.
  • Age : Age of customer (numeric)
  • Job : Type of job (categorical: “admin.”,“unknown”,“unemployed”,“management”,“housemaid”,“entrepreneur”,“student”, “blue-collar”, “self-employed”, “retired”, “technician”, “services”)
  • Marital : Marital status (categorical: “married”, “divorced”, “single”; note: “divorced” means divorced or widowed)
  • Education : Education of customer (categorical: “unknown”, “secondary”, “primary”, “tertiary”)
  • Default: Has credit in default? (binary: “yes”, “no”)
  • Balance: Average yearly balance, in euros (numeric)
  • Housing: Does customer has housing loan? (binary: “yes”, “no”)
  • Loan: Does customer has personal loan? (binary: “yes”, “no”)

Related with the last contact of the current campaign

  • Contact: Contact communication type (categorical: “unknown”, “telephone”, “cellular”)
  • Day: Last contact day of the month (numeric))

    • Direct Marketing Campaign: Details and Phase I Tasks

  • Month: Last contact month of year (categorical: “jan”, “feb”, “mar”, . . . , “nov”, “dec”)
  • Duration: Last contact duration, in seconds (numeric)

    • Other attributes

  • Campaign: Number of contacts performed during this campaign and for this client (numeric, includes last contact)
  • Pdays: Number of days that passed by after the client was last contacted from a previous campaign (numeric, -1 means client was not previously contacted)
  • Previous: Number of contacts performed before this campaign and for this client (numeric)
  • Poutcome: Outcome of the previous marketing campaign (categorical: “unknown”, “other”, “failure”, “success”)

    • Objective :

    Our main objective is to identify as many as potential customers who would likely to subscribe for new deposit product.
    We will use "Accuracy" matrix as one of the matrix to judge our results. Also data is highly imbalanced, so other matrix we will check is “True Positive Rate (Recall)” ,"Precession" and "KS".

    Results and Conclusion :

    Results :

    Accuracy of ~90% on test data
    KS score of ~42% on test data
    Precession of ~62% on test data
    True Positive Rate of ~46% on test data

    Conclusion :

    With Accuracy of 90% we can say we are able to identify (segregate) most of our customers accurately in terms of which customer would likely to subscribe for deposit product.
    As data is highly imbalanced, true picture of our results is provided by precession rate, which is 62%, i.e. out of total identified potential customers 62% are likely to subscribe for deposit product.
    With True Positive rate of 46% we can say we are able to capture 46% of total actual potential customer who are likely to subscribe for deposit product.

    Final comments:

    So to summarize, looking at the above results one may say we are loosing around 54% of our potential customer, but with this model now manager would call only 10% customer from the available list so, this will reduce cost, most importantly as we are calling less number of customer, there would not be any customer dissatisfaction about unnecessary marketing calls.
    We must remember not model can be perfect and provide 100% results, and there is always a chance of improvement.
    Also, we can see from this results that, there is a tradeoff between 54% loosing potential customer and decreasing customer dissatisfaction.
    So by choosing this model manager would do a good job as he has maintain the satisfaction level 90% of his costumer in list by not making unnecessary calls.

    Note:

    I have used Xgboost on this data set and I'm able to achieve True positive rate of 82%. So by using different algorithm is possible to achieve higher TPR. But for demonstration purpose I have used random forest.
